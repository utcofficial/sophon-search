Toyota is a Japanese multinational automotive manufacturer headquartered in Toyota City, Aichi, Japan. It was founded by Kiichiro Toyoda and incorporated on August 28, 1937. Toyota is one of the largest automobile manufacturers in the world, producing about 10 million vehicles per year. The company offers a wide range of vehicles including the Corolla, Camry, RAV4, Land Cruiser, Prius, and Highlander. Toyota is known for its reliable vehicles, innovative manufacturing processes like the Toyota Production System, and leadership in hybrid technology with the Prius.

Honda is a Japanese public multinational conglomerate manufacturer of automobiles, motorcycles, and power equipment. It has been the world's largest motorcycle manufacturer since 1959, as well as the world's largest manufacturer of internal combustion engines measured by volume, producing more than 14 million internal combustion engines each year. Honda's automotive lineup includes the Civic, Accord, CR-V, Pilot, and Odyssey. The company is known for engineering excellence, fuel-efficient vehicles, and the VTEC engine technology.

Ford is an American multinational automaker founded by Henry Ford and incorporated on June 16, 1903. The company sells automobiles and commercial vehicles under the Ford brand, and luxury cars under its Lincoln luxury brand. Ford is known for revolutionizing manufacturing with the moving assembly line, making cars affordable for the masses with the Model T. Current models include the F-150 pickup truck, Mustang, Explorer, Escape, and Expedition. Ford is a leader in truck sales in North America.

BMW is a German multinational manufacturer of luxury vehicles and motorcycles headquartered in Munich, Bavaria. The company was founded in 1916 as a manufacturer of aircraft engines, which it produced from 1917 to 1918 and again from 1933 to 1945. BMW is known for its performance-oriented vehicles, distinctive kidney grille design, and the Ultimate Driving Machine marketing slogan. Models include the 3 Series, 5 Series, 7 Series, X3, X5, and the sports car Z4. BMW also owns Mini and Rolls-Royce brands.

Mercedes-Benz is a German global automobile marque and a division of Daimler AG. The brand is known for luxury vehicles, buses, coaches, and trucks. The headquarters is in Stuttgart, Baden-Württemberg. Mercedes-Benz is one of the most recognized automotive brands worldwide. The company is known for its innovative safety features, luxury interiors, and performance vehicles including the C-Class, E-Class, S-Class, GLC, GLE, and the iconic G-Class off-road vehicle.

Audi is a German automotive manufacturer of luxury vehicles headquartered in Ingolstadt, Bavaria. Audi is a member of the Volkswagen Group and has its roots at Zwickau, currently in Germany. The company name is based on the Latin translation of the surname of the founder, August Horch. Audi is known for its Quattro all-wheel drive system, advanced technology, and progressive design. Models include the A3, A4, A6, A8, Q3, Q5, Q7, and the R8 sports car.

Porsche is a German automobile manufacturer specializing in high-performance sports cars, SUVs, and sedans. The company was founded in 1931 by Ferdinand Porsche. Porsche's most famous model is the 911, a rear-engine sports car that has been in continuous production since 1963. Other models include the Boxster, Cayman, Cayenne SUV, Macan compact SUV, and the Panamera sedan. Porsche is known for engineering excellence, distinctive design, and racing heritage.

Ferrari is an Italian luxury sports car manufacturer based in Maranello, Italy. Founded by Enzo Ferrari in 1939, the company built its first car in 1940. Ferrari is known for its participation in racing, especially in Formula One, where it is the oldest and most successful team. Ferrari produces some of the world's most desirable supercars including the F8 Tributo, SF90 Stradale, Roma, and limited edition models like the LaFerrari. Ferraris are known for their powerful V8 and V12 engines, stunning design, and exclusivity.

Lamborghini is an Italian manufacturer of luxury sports cars and SUVs based in Sant'Agata Bolognese. The company was founded in 1963 by Ferruccio Lamborghini to compete with established marques like Ferrari. Lamborghini is known for its aggressive, angular designs, powerful V12 and V10 engines, and exotic models including the Aventador, Huracán, Urus SUV, and limited production models like the Sián. The company's logo features a charging bull, reflecting founder Ferruccio's zodiac sign.

Tesla is an American electric vehicle and clean energy company based in Austin, Texas. Tesla designs and manufactures electric cars, battery energy storage, solar panels, and related products. Tesla is one of the world's most valuable companies and the world's most valuable automaker. Models include the Model S sedan, Model 3 compact sedan, Model X SUV, Model Y compact SUV, Cybertruck, and Roadster sports car. Tesla is known for its long-range electric vehicles, Autopilot driver assistance, and over-the-air software updates.
