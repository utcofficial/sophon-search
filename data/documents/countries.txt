Vanuatu covers an area of 12,189 square kilometers and has a population of over 320,000. The capital and largest city is Port Vila, on the island of Efate. The official languages are Bislama, English, and French. The currency is the Vanuatu Vatu (VUV). Vanuatu is a parliamentary republic with a President as head of state and a Prime Minister as head of government. Vanuatu is known for its stunning beaches, its active volcanoes (Mount Yasur), its traditional cultures (including land diving on Pentecost Island), and its vulnerability to cyclones and earthquakes.

New Caledonia is a special collectivity of France in the southwest Pacific Ocean, located 1,210 kilometers east of Australia and 16,136 kilometers east of Metropolitan France. It comprises the main island of Grande Terre, the Loyalty Islands, and several smaller islands. New Caledonia covers an area of 18,576 square kilometers and has a population of over 293,000. The capital and largest city is Nouméa. The official language is French. The currency is the CFP Franc (XPF). New Caledonia is known for its stunning lagoon (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), its biodiversity, its nickel mining, and its ongoing debate over independence from France.

French Polynesia is an overseas collectivity of France in the South Pacific Ocean. It comprises 121 geographically dispersed islands and atolls, including the famous Society Islands (Tahiti, Bora Bora) and the Marquesas Islands. French Polynesia covers an area of 4,167 square kilometers (land area) spread over 2,000 kilometers of ocean. It has a population of over 300,000. The capital and largest city is Papeete, on Tahiti. The official language is French. The currency is the CFP Franc (XPF). French Polynesia is known for its stunning overwater bungalows, its turquoise lagoons, its vibrant Polynesian culture, and its status as a popular luxury tourist destination.

Samoa is a country in Oceania, consisting of two main islands, Upolu and Savai'i, and several smaller islands, located in the central South Pacific Ocean. Samoa covers an area of 2,842 square kilometers and has a population of over 218,000. The capital and largest city is Apia, on Upolu. The official languages are Samoan and English. The currency is the Samoan Tālā (WST). Samoa is a parliamentary republic with a Prime Minister as head of government and a Head of State (O le Ao o le Malo) as ceremonial head. Samoa is known for its stunning beaches, its traditional Fa'a Samoa culture, its rugby prowess, and its position as one of the first countries to see the new day.

Tonga is a country in Oceania, consisting of an archipelago of 169 islands, of which 36 are inhabited, in the South Pacific Ocean. Tonga covers an area of 748 square kilometers and has a population of over 104,000. The capital and largest city is Nuku'alofa, on the island of Tongatapu. The official languages are Tongan and English. The currency is the Tongan Pa'anga (TOP). Tonga is a constitutional monarchy with King Tupou VI as head of state and a Prime Minister as head of government. Tonga is known as the "Friendly Islands," its stunning beaches, its unique culture, and its status as the only remaining monarchy in the Pacific.

Kiribati is a country in Oceania, consisting of 32 atolls and one raised coral island (Banaba) dispersed over 3.5 million square kilometers of the central Pacific Ocean. Kiribati covers a land area of just 811 square kilometers but has an Exclusive Economic Zone of 3.5 million square kilometers. It has a population of over 133,000. The capital and largest city is South Tarawa, on the Tarawa Atoll. The official languages are English and Gilbertese. The currency is the Kiribati Dollar (KID) and Australian Dollar (AUD). Kiribati is a parliamentary republic with a President as head of state and government. Kiribati is known for its low-lying atolls, its vulnerability to climate change and sea-level rise, its rich marine resources, and its unique position straddling the equator and the International Date Line.

Tuvalu is a country in Oceania, consisting of nine islands and atolls in the South Pacific Ocean, midway between Hawaii and Australia. Tuvalu covers a land area of just 26 square kilometers, making it the fourth-smallest country in the world. It has a population of over 11,000. The capital and largest city is Funafuti. The official languages are Tuvaluan and English. The currency is the Tuvaluan Dollar (TVD) and Australian Dollar (AUD). Tuvalu is a parliamentary constitutional monarchy with King Charles III as head of state and a Governor-General representing the monarch, and a Prime Minister as head of government. Tuvalu is known for its extreme vulnerability to climate change and sea-level rise, its stunning lagoon, and its traditional Polynesian culture.

Nauru is a microstate in Oceania, consisting of a single island in the central Pacific Ocean. It is the third-smallest country by area, after Vatican City and Monaco, covering just 21 square kilometers. Nauru has a population of over 10,000. The capital and largest city is Yaren (though Nauru has no official capital). The official languages are Nauruan and English. The currency is the Australian Dollar (AUD). Nauru is a parliamentary republic with a President as head of state and government. Nauru is known for its history of phosphate mining, which has left much of the island uninhabitable, its "fat" period of wealth in the 1970s and 1980s, and its current role as a detention center for asylum seekers.

Marshall Islands, officially the Republic of the Marshall Islands, is a country in Oceania, consisting of 29 atolls and five isolated islands in the central Pacific Ocean. The islands are located north of Kiribati and east of the Federated States of Micronesia. The Marshall Islands covers a land area of 181 square kilometers and has a population of over 58,000. The capital and largest city is Majuro. The official languages are Marshallese and English. The currency is the United States Dollar (USD). The Marshall Islands is a presidential republic in free association with the United States. The Marshall Islands is known for its stunning coral atolls, its role as a site for US nuclear testing (Bikini Atoll), its vulnerability to climate change, and its unique traditional navigation techniques.

Palau is a country in Oceania, consisting of an archipelago of over 500 islands in the western Pacific Ocean. It is located east of the Philippines and north of Indonesia. Palau covers a land area of 459 square kilometers and has a population of over 18,000. The capital is Ngerulmud, and the largest city is Koror. The official languages are Palauan and English. The currency is the United States Dollar (USD). Palau is a presidential republic in free association with the United States. Palau is known for its stunning Rock Islands, its incredible marine biodiversity, its jellyfish lake, and its commitment to conservation (it has created one of the world's largest shark sanctuaries).

Federated States of Micronesia is a country in Oceania, consisting of four states (Yap, Chuuk, Pohnpei, and Kosrae) spread across the western Pacific Ocean. It comprises over 600 islands covering a land area of 702 square kilometers. The FSM has a population of over 113,000. The capital is Palikir, on Pohnpei. The official language is English. The currency is the United States Dollar (USD). The FSM is a federal presidential republic in free association with the United States. The FSM is known for its stunning coral reefs, its traditional cultures (including the stone money of Yap), its World War II wrecks (Chuuk Lagoon), and its unique archaeological sites (Nan Madol).

Maldives, officially the Republic of Maldives, is an archipelagic country in the Indian Ocean, located southwest of Sri Lanka and India. It consists of 26 atolls, which are made up of more than 1,000 coral islands. It is one of the world's most geographically dispersed countries and the smallest Asian country by land area, covering just 298 square kilometers. It has a population of over 521,000. The capital and largest city is Malé. The official language is Dhivehi. The currency is the Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR). Maldives is a presidential republic with a President as head of state and government. Maldives is known for its stunning luxury resorts, its overwater bungalows, its incredible marine life, and its extreme vulnerability to climate change and sea-level rise.

Bhutan, officially the Kingdom of Bhutan, is a landlocked country in the Eastern Himalayas in South Asia. It is bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west. Bhutan covers an area of 38,394 square kilometers and has a population of over 777,000. The capital and largest city is Thimphu. The official language is Dzongkha. The currency is the Bhutanese Ngultrum (BTN). Bhutan is a constitutional monarchy with King Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck as head of state and a Prime Minister as head of government. Bhutan is known for its philosophy of Gross National Happiness (GNH) over Gross Domestic Product, its stunning Himalayan landscapes, its ancient Buddhist monasteries (including the iconic Tiger's Nest), and its traditional dress and architecture.

Nepal, as mentioned, is a landlocked country in the Himalayas, known for Mount Everest and its rich Hindu and Buddhist culture.

Bangladesh, as mentioned, is a densely populated country in South Asia, known for its rivers, the Sundarbans, and its textile industry.

Myanmar, as mentioned, is a country in Southeast Asia, known for its ancient temples (Bagan) and its political transition.

Laos, officially the Lao People's Democratic Republic, is a landlocked country in Southeast Asia, bordered by Myanmar and China to the northwest, Vietnam to the east, Cambodia to the southeast, and Thailand to the west. Laos covers an area of 237,955 square kilometers and has a population of over 7.6 million. The capital and largest city is Vientiane. The official language is Lao. The currency is the Lao Kip (LAK).
