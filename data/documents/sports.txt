Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field at the centre of which is a 22-yard pitch with a wicket at each end, each comprising two bails balanced on three stumps. The game proceeds when a player on the fielding team, called the bowler, bowls the ball from one end of the pitch towards the wicket at the other end. Cricket is one of the most popular sports in the world, particularly in countries like India, Australia, England, Pakistan, South Africa, and the West Indies. Major formats include Test cricket (5 days), One Day Internationals (50 overs), and Twenty20 (20 overs). The Indian Premier League is one of the most-watched cricket leagues globally.

Football, also known as soccer in some countries, is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players who primarily use their feet to propel a ball around a rectangular field called a pitch. The objective of the game is to score more goals than the opponent by moving the ball beyond the goal line into the opposing goal. Football is the world's most popular sport, played by over 250 million players in over 200 countries. Major competitions include the FIFA World Cup, UEFA Champions League, and domestic leagues like the English Premier League, La Liga, and Serie A. Famous players include Pelé, Diego Maradona, Lionel Messi, and Cristiano Ronaldo.

Basketball is a team sport in which two teams, most commonly of five players each, opposing one another on a rectangular court, compete with the primary objective of shooting a basketball through the defender's hoop while preventing the opposing team from shooting through their own hoop. Basketball is one of the world's most popular and widely viewed sports. The NBA is the premier professional basketball league in the world. Famous players include Michael Jordan, LeBron James, Kobe Bryant, and Stephen Curry.

Tennis is a racket sport that is played either individually against a single opponent or between two teams of two players each. Each player uses a tennis racket that is strung with cord to strike a hollow rubber ball covered with felt over or around a net and into the opponent's court. Tennis is an Olympic sport and is played at all levels of society and at all ages. Major tournaments include the four Grand Slams: Australian Open, French Open, Wimbledon, and US Open. Famous players include Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic, and Serena Williams.

Formula One is the highest class of international racing for open-wheel single-seater formula racing cars sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile. The F1 world championship season consists of a series of races, known as Grands Prix, held usually on purpose-built circuits, and in a few cases on closed city streets. F1 cars are the fastest regulated road-course racing cars in the world. Famous teams include Ferrari, Mercedes, Red Bull, and McLaren. Famous drivers include Michael Schumacher, Lewis Hamilton, Ayrton Senna, and Max Verstappen.

Hockey is a sport in which two teams play against each other by trying to manoeuvre a ball or a puck into the opponent's goal using a hockey stick. There are many types of hockey such as bandy, field hockey, ice hockey and rink hockey. Field hockey is played on grass or turf and is popular in countries like India, Pakistan, Netherlands, and Australia. Ice hockey is played on ice and is popular in Canada, United States, Russia, and Scandinavian countries.

Golf is a club-and-ball sport in which players use various clubs to hit balls into a series of holes on a course in as few strokes as possible. Golf, unlike most ball games, cannot and does not utilize a standardized playing area. The game is played on a course with an arranged progression of holes, each with a teeing ground, fairway, rough, and other hazards, and a green with a cylindrical hole. Major tournaments include the Masters, U.S. Open, The Open Championship, and PGA Championship. Famous golfers include Tiger Woods, Jack Nicklaus, and Arnold Palmer.

Boxing is a combat sport in which two people, usually wearing protective gloves and other protective equipment such as hand wraps and mouthguards, throw punches at each other for a predetermined amount of time in a boxing ring. Boxing is one of the oldest combat sports, with evidence of similar sports in ancient Egypt and Greece. Professional boxing is governed by organizations like the WBA, WBC, IBF, and WBO. Famous boxers include Muhammad Ali, Mike Tyson, Joe Louis, and Manny Pacquiao.

Rugby is a contact team sport that originated in England in the first half of the 19th century. Rugby is played with an oval ball on a grass field. There are two main types of rugby: rugby union and rugby league. Rugby union has 15 players per side and is played in over 100 countries, particularly in New Zealand, South Africa, England, Australia, and France. The Rugby World Cup is the premier international competition. Rugby sevens, with seven players per side, became an Olympic sport in 2016.

Badminton is a racquet sport played using racquets to hit a shuttlecock across a net. It is played with two opposing players (singles) or two opposing pairs (doubles). Points are scored by landing the shuttlecock on the opponent's side of the court. Badminton is an Olympic sport with five disciplines: men's singles, women's singles, men's doubles, women's doubles, and mixed doubles. It is particularly popular in Asian countries like China, Indonesia, India, Malaysia, and South Korea. Famous players include Lin Dan, Lee Chong Wei, and Saina Nehwal.

Minecraft is a sandbox video game developed by Mojang Studios. The game was created by Markus Persson in the Java programming language. Following several early private testing versions, it was first made public in May 2009 before fully releasing in November 2011, with Jens Bergensten then taking over development. Minecraft has become the best-selling video game in history, with over 300 million copies sold. In Minecraft, players explore a blocky, procedurally generated 3D world, and may discover and extract raw materials, craft tools, build structures, and survive depending on game mode.

Fortnite is an online video game developed by Epic Games and released in 2017. It is available in three distinct game mode versions that otherwise share the same general gameplay and game engine: Fortnite Battle Royale, a free-to-play battle royale game in which up to 100 players fight to be the last person standing; Fortnite: Save the World, a cooperative hybrid tower defense-shooter and survival game in which up to four players fight off zombie-like creatures and defend objects with traps and fortifications; and Fortnite Creative, in which players are given complete freedom to create worlds and battle arenas.

Call of Duty is a first-person shooter video game franchise published by Activision. Starting out in 2003, it first focused on games set in World War II. Over time, the series has seen games set in the midst of the Cold War, futuristic worlds, and the modern day. The series has sold over 400 million copies and has over 100 million monthly active players. The franchise includes sub-series like Modern Warfare, Black Ops, and Warzone, a free-to-play battle royale. Games are known for their cinematic campaigns, competitive multiplayer, and cooperative Zombies modes.

Grand Theft Auto is an action-adventure game series created by David Jones and Mike Dailly and later developed by Rockstar Games. The series is set in fictional locales modelled after real-life cities, usually within the United States, and follows criminals trying to rise through the ranks of the underworld. The gameplay focuses on an open world where the player can complete missions to progress an overall story, as well as engage in various side activities. Grand Theft Auto V is one of the best-selling games of all time, with over 185 million copies sold.

The Legend of Zelda is a fantasy action-adventure video game franchise created by Japanese game designers Shigeru Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka. It is primarily developed and published by Nintendo. The gameplay incorporates action, adventure, and puzzle-solving elements. The series centers on Link, the playable character, who is often tasked with rescuing Princess Zelda and defeating the primary antagonist Ganon. The franchise includes critically acclaimed titles like Ocarina of Time, Breath of the Wild, and Tears of the Kingdom.

Pokémon is a media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori in 1995, managed by The Pokémon Company, and primarily focused on video games developed by Game Freak. The franchise is set in a world where humans live alongside creatures called Pokémon. Players take on the role of Pokémon Trainers, with the goal of catching and training Pokémon to battle other trainers. Pokémon has become the highest-grossing media franchise of all time, with games, anime, movies, trading card game, and merchandise. Major games include Pokémon Red/Blue, Gold/Silver, and recent titles like Scarlet/Violet.

FIFA is a series of association football video games developed and released annually by Electronic Arts under the EA Sports label. The series has sold over 325 million copies, making it one of the best-selling video game franchises. The games simulate real-world football, featuring licensed leagues, teams, and players from around the world. Gameplay includes career modes, ultimate team building, and online multiplayer. The series is known for its realistic graphics, commentary, and gameplay mechanics. In 2023, the series was rebranded as EA Sports FC after EA's partnership with FIFA ended.

Super Mario is a platform game series created by Nintendo starring their mascot, Mario. It is the central series of the greater Mario franchise. At least one Super Mario game has been released for every major Nintendo video game console. The games follow Mario's adventures, typically in the fictional Mushroom Kingdom, where he often battles his arch-enemy Bowser and rescues Princess Peach. Super Mario games are known for their creative level design, tight controls, and memorable music. Major titles include Super Mario Bros., Super Mario 64, Super Mario Odyssey, and Super Mario Wonder.

Red Dead Redemption is a series of Western-themed action-adventure games published by Rockstar Games. The series consists of two main games: Red Dead Revolver (2004) and the critically acclaimed Red Dead Redemption (2010) and Red Dead Redemption 2 (2018). The games are set in the declining years of the American Wild West in the early 20th century. Players take on the roles of outlaws navigating a changing world. The series is praised for its storytelling, immersive open world, attention to detail, and multiplayer modes.

Among Us is an online multiplayer social deduction game developed and published by American game studio Innersloth. It was released in 2018 but gained massive popularity in 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic. The game takes place in a space-themed setting where players are assigned roles as either Crewmates or Impostors. Crewmates must complete tasks around the map while identifying and voting out the Impostors, who sabotage the ship and kill Crewmates. The game is known for its simple graphics, social gameplay, and deception mechanics.
